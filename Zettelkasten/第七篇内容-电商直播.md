## üåç Why Hasn't It Taken Off In The West?

Good question. Here's my analysis:

**Cultural Factors**:
- China: Group shopping experience is normal
- West: Privacy concerns with live video
- China: Influencer trust is high
- West: Influencer skepticism ("it's a scam")

**Infrastructure**:
- China: 2-hour delivery standard in major cities
- West: 2-day delivery (Amazon Prime)
- China: Mobile-first, everything in one app
- West: Fragmented apps, fragmented payments

**Platform Differences**:
- Douyin: Integrated e-commerce from Day 1
- TikTok (West): Initially entertainment-only
- China: WeChat/Alipay everywhere
- West: Credit cards, PayPal, Apple Pay (less friction?)

**Regulation**:
- China: Fast approval for live-commerce
- West: Stricter regulations on product claims
- China: Less consumer protection (sometimes)
- West: More scrutiny of influencer marketing
