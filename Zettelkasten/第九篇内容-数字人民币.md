## üéØ How It's Different From What You Use

**This isn't Bitcoin. This isn't crypto.**

**Digital Yuan (e-CNY)**:
- Central bank-issued (People's Bank of China)
- Stable: 1 e-CNY = 1 yuan (no volatility)
- Privacy: Anonymous small transactions, traceable large ones
- Offline: Works without internet (NFC technology)
- Universal: Everyone must accept it (legal tender)

**Bitcoin/Cryptocurrency**:
- Decentralized
- Volatile (price swings wildly)
- Anonymous (but traceable on blockchain)
- Requires internet
- Not legal tender (merchants can refuse)

**Western Mobile Payments (Apple Pay, Venmo)**:
- Still linked to bank accounts/cards
- Transaction fees: 2-3%
- Settlement time: 1-3 business days
- Not legal tender (merchants can refuse)
- Offline: Limited or no functionality

- Settlement time: 1-3 business days

**Digital Yuan Costs**:
- Consumer transactions: 0% fee
- Merchant transactions: 0% fee (for most transactions)
- Bank transfers: 0% fee
- International transfers: Minimal fees (still being developed)
- Settlement time: Instant (real-time)

**Who Benefits?**
- **Consumers**: No fees, instant transfers
- **Small merchants**: No processing fees, no equipment costs
- **Government**: Transparency, reduces corruption, easier taxation
- **Banks**: Lower operational costs (but lower fee revenue)

## üîí Privacy vs Transparency Balance

**How it works**:
- **Small transactions** (under 2,000 yuan/$280): Anonymous
- **Medium transactions** (2,000-50,000 yuan/$7,000): Semi-anonymous
- **Large transactions** (over 50,000 yuan): Fully traceable

**Why this balance?**
- **Privacy**: Normal daily purchases aren't tracked
- **Anti-corruption**: Large movements can be monitored
- **Anti-money laundering**: Suspicious patterns flagged
- **Crime prevention**: Harder to use for illegal activities

**Comparison**:
- **Cash**: Fully anonymous (but inconvenient, risky)
- **Credit cards**: Fully tracked by banks and companies
- **Digital yuan**: Balanced (privacy for small, transparency for large)
